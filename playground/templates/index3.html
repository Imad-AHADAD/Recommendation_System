Risk Recommendations
Technical risk
  • Carry out an analysis of the software's complexity at the very start of the project to anticipate design and maintenance challenges.
  • Assess the software's functional and non-functional requirements.
  • Define a solid architecture and use static analysis tools to avoid duplication of code.
  • Test the software regularly and implement regular security tests throughout development to identify and correct vulnerabilities.
  • Use automated vulnerability scanning tools like Burp Suite, OWASP ZAP, etc.
  • Add tools such as Jira, Trello, or Bugzilla to track detected vulnerabilities and their correction status.
  • Ensure effective communication between the members of the development team to quickly resolve design and implementation problems.
The risks associated with project management
  • Plan the project realistically, taking into account the workload of each member of the development team.
  • Define project objectives, identify tasks, estimate task duration, determine dependencies, and monitor progress.
  • Establish clear and regular communication with stakeholders to maintain a common vision of the project.
  • Assess the project scope on an ongoing basis and revise it accordingly to avoid spill-over.
  • Set up training courses for team members on risk management, time management, and conflict management.
Commercial risks
  • Draw up a realistic budget and carry out regular monitoring to avoid cost overruns.
  • Involve stakeholders in defining the project to meet their expectations.
  • Monitor market trends to anticipate changes and adapt software development accordingly
  • Establish clear policies for managing conflicts and issues, including protocols for resolving conflicts between team members and with external stakeholders.
Human risks
  • Recruit members of the development team with the skills needed to meet the project's technical and organizational challenges.
  • Establish a training plan for members of the development team to enhance their skills.
  • Maintain a positive working environment to motivate the team and reduce turnover.
  • Offer development opportunities to team members to strengthen their commitment and motivation.
